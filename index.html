the night market set
it's two weeks before the end of March and i'm trying to think about my next dj set so that i can keep up with the one-mix-a-month rule that i set for myself. i wanted to keep myself accountable on remaining consisent with my djing hobby. my friend asks me to dj for his "night market" event, so it really worked out because it definitely boosts my motivation to go out there a perform my sets and not just make some and play them in my bedroom. the event rules were pretty cool. if you haven't read "the art of the gathering" and wanted to learn how to make successful, more inclusive events, i'd recommend the summary at least bc i didn't read it, only the summary üòù. but i think the suggestions it provided are very useful to know when planning a gathering that's more than just a party.

RULES OF THE MARKET
1. Bring a bag of goodies
2. Trade, barter, & haggle (anything but money)
3. Enjoy a lineup of 6 deadly DJs
4. First trade happens at the door!

I traded some solar eclipse glasses and various flavors of mintia and ended up with a casette tape (i still gotta find a casette player), an MW hat (a brand from a movie called Beau is Afraid), an origami crane.



Now onto the set...


i sometimes find it difficult to convey how i feel. there's sometimes a
l
a

g between experiencing the feeling and the moment that i'm trying to express it ((suppression)). in my past dj sets, i've mostly been focused on the technical aspect of the music -- the groove, the harmonics, making loops that sound good on top of other loops and the transitions between the songs. this time, i made this set with the intention of focusing on the emotional aspect of the music and feelings that i have longed to express. everyone interprets, feels, and reacts to music differently but i wanted to guide the listener on a journey by incorporating a theme. this set reflects some emotions that i felt in the past and sometimes felt like i experience them again and again and again and again and again and again. i won't try to explain away those feelings with words here because i think that defeats the purpose of the set.

i've found that playing songs that have synth pads are suuuper expressive for me which is really nice. and i also find spoken word and vocals which almost sound like synth pads, like you can barely make out the words that are in the song, but you can still feel the emotion behind it is very powerful. i also tried experimenting with slowing down songs a bunch to see if it could sound synthy with some reverb applied.

i'm noticing a pattern in the way i make my sets. i have a core song that i use as the anchor. this one is "us ephemeral". i usually think of this core song as the center -- something that i want to build towards and then after give the listener a lingering feeling of that song in the songs that follow. kind of repeating some elements later from this core song. for this one, i wanted to start out with more ambient, melancholic songs. then building up some energy with the more upbeat songs. "fleeting frozen heart" has a very nice piano riff mixed with some chill drum and bass, which i think fit well to give the listener a bit of pause but also maintaining some energy. "comfort zone" i didn't really feel like the lyrics fit the theme but the synth pad is great. "loving is easy" i included this track because it represents confusion to me. i wanted to end with a soft, optimistic-leaning euphoria so "dhollandia" went in. i really like the vocals, the lil zaps and the accents on the beat near the end.

i've included the pre-recording and the live set, which has the mistakes and all (unintended faster BPMs) and an extra song bc i had to fill until the next dj came.
